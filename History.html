History of the Companions

Origins

Origins of the Companions can be traced from the Five Hundred Companions, the army Ysgramor gathered to avenge the Night of Tears. After winning the war, the remnants of the Five Hundred Companions went their own ways, traversing the unexplored lands of Tamriel.This period was known as The Return.

Hundreds of years after the passing of Ysgramor, the Companions had become soldiers for hire. Their services could be purchased for the purpose of fighting in wars, this often meant that Shield-Brothers could be forced to fight on opposite sides. This conflict of interest was jeopardizing their renown sense of honor. In his wisdom, Harbinger Mryfwiil, decided they would no longer take part in any war or other political conflicts. Because of this decision, the Companions are now known as impartial arbiters of honor, in addition to their triumphs in battle.

By the end of the First Era, the Companions had started doing away with the rigid rules of Atmoran purity and were recruiting people of all races and creeds to join their ranks. After dark periods in the late Second Era, when a string of false Harbingers laid claim to Jorrvaskr, it was Kyrnil Long-Nose who rallied the true hearts of the Companions in the wilds. Kyrnil and the Companions stormed Jorrvaskr, killing the usurpers and returning honor through blood. He began the tradition of trusted advisors called The Circle (named after Ysgramor's council of captains) who would serve as examples to the younger, newer Companions.

Jorrvaskr

According to legend, Jorrvaskr is the oldest building in Whiterun. It once existed by itself on the mountain, with the rest of the town springing up around it over the following centuries. Jorrvaskr was originally a longboat made for the Return of the Five Hundred Companions of Ysgramor. Its captain was Jeek of the River, and its crew only consisted of twenty two men.

Upon arrival in Skyrim, Ysgramor commanded that his men go forth and settle in this new land. Jeek of the River and his crew traveled southwards towards the area that was later named Whiterun Hold.

It was there that they discovered a strange monument that later became known as the Skyforge. Jeek and his crew decided that the mysterious Skyforge could be of use to them. The unscathed land surrounding the Skyforge was fit for harvest, and so began the labors upon the Atmoran wood that had born them all across the sea, their boat, Jorrvaskr, became their shelter.
